More than 45 per cent of girls in India don't go to school. Here's why

Challenges

India is a country with more than one billion people, and just one-third of them can read. Rapidly growing size of population, shortages of teachers, books, and basic facilities, and insufficient public funds to cover education costs are some of the nation’s toughest challenges.


Gender Inequality

In 2011, the disparity in access to education between genders was most visible in India’s childhood literacy rates – 82% of boys were literate while only 65% of girls could read and write. NGO Plan India carried out a study that showed that 23% of girls in India leave school once they hit puberty. And, the girls who continue their education miss as many as 50 school days each year as a result of menstruation.


Child Marriage

A girl is more likely to drop out of school during the preparatory time before her marriage or shortly after.Child marriage often results in adolescent pregnancy. A pregnant adolescent girl may drop out or be excluded from school because of national laws, a lack of support for re-enrolment and stigma


Violence

Violence also discourage females from enrolling in school or attending at all. Every year, around 60 million girls are sexually abused while travelling to school. As a result, attendance declines and dropout rates rise.
